Temporal Lobe

  • Sensory functions: integrate and interpret sensory input (audio & visual), long-term memory and spatial awareness (in the hippocampus, situated in the temporal lobe), language comprehension, personality and behaviour.
  • Damage to the temporal lobe: may be able to see/hear stimuli but not categorize or name it
  • Located near base of the brain
  • Damage can affect senses, memory or personality
  • Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s can cause changes in the temporal lobe (destroy cells in hippocampus = memory difficulties)

Temporal Lobe Epilepsy

  • Can result from brain injury, seizure or fever = a better-known source of temporal lobe damage
  • Can cause longer-term damage
  • Temporal lobe seizures can affect personality & cause: Paranoid behaviour rage /aggression Altered sexual behaviour Perseverative speech
  • May feel nothing, or intense fright or pleasure, or experience old memories or foreign emotions
  • Major depression, dysthymia, anxiety disorders
  • Personality assessment could indicate disease severity
  • WHO estimates that 50 million people worldwide have epilepsy (one of the most common neurological disorders)
  • Psychiatric comorbidities are frequent in epilepsy
  • Onset of epilepsy before the age of 10 is more commonly linked with psychosis

Alzheimer's and how to treat it

  • WHAT IS IT?: Most common cause of dementia in the UK,Ongoing decline of brain function affecting memory, thinking competency and other mental abilities
  • RISKS: Increasing age, Family history, Untreated depression, Lifestyle factors and conditions associated with cardiovascular disease
  • SYMPTOMS: Progressive Minor memory problems (first sign) Developes to confusion, disorientation Difficulty making decisions Speech & language problems Issues with self-care Personality changes (aggression, suspicion and demanding) Hallucinations & delusions Low mood/anxiety.
  • TREATMENT: currently no cure. Medicines for symptomatic relief, Changes to home environment to aid independence Psychological interventions e.g., cognitive stimulation therapy
  • PREVENTATION: Stop smoking, cut down on alcohol Healthy, balanced diet, maintain a healthy weight Stay physically fit and mentally active
