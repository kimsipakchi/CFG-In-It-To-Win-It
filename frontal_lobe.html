What Is Depression?

  • Depression (a Major depression disorder) is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how you feel, the way you think and how you act.

It is treatable!

  • Can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems and can decrease the ability to function at work and at home
  • Can occur at any time, but on average, first appears during the late teens to mid-20s Women are more likely to experience depression
  • Symptoms can vary from mild to severe Feeling sad or having a depressed mood, Loss of interest or pleasure in activities once enjoyed,Changes in appetite,Trouble sleeping/sleeping too much,Loss of energy or increased fatigue,Increase in purposeless physical activity/ slowed movements or speech, Feeling worthless or guilty Difficulty thinking, concentrating or making decisions,Thoughts of death or suicide
  • Several factors can play a role in depression: Biochemistry, Genetics, Personality, Environmental factors, Possible treatment, Self-help and coping, Medication, Psychotherapy and Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)
